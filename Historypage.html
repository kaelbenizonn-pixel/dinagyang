History of the Dinagyang Festival

The Dinagyang Festival began in 1967 when a replica of the Santo Niño was brought from Cebu to Iloilo City. Inspired by the Ati-Atihan Festival of Aklan, Iloilo developed its own version of the celebration.

In 1977, the festival officially adopted the name "Dinagyang," which means "merrymaking" in the local Hiligaynon language. The festival evolved into a highly competitive event where tribes perform choreographed dances portraying the historic purchase of Panay Island and honoring the Santo Niño.

Over the years, the Dinagyang Festival has grown into an internationally recognized event, earning awards for tourism and cultural preservation.
